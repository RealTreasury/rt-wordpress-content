What Are Cash Tools?

The foundational category of treasury management systems designed for companies with simple cash structures and straightforward needs

Essential Cash Management Foundation

Cash Tools provide a single portal to view your bank account balances and transactions while creating basic forecasts. These solutions serve as the entry point into automated treasury management, offering the four core functions every business needs to gain control over their cash operations.

Perfect for organizations that need to move beyond spreadsheets and manual processes, Cash Tools deliver bank connectivity, forecasting capabilities, global cash visibility, and transaction management in one integrated platform. Companies requiring more advanced functionality like treasury payments, market data feeds, or derivatives management typically graduate to TMS-LITE or full Treasury & Risk Management Systems.

Key Insight: A product must have ALL four required functions to qualify as a complete Cash Tool. Missing any core function means it's either incomplete or belongs in a different category.

The 4 Core Cash Tool Functions

Every cash management system must include these essential capabilities to qualify as a complete Cash Tool solution

🔗 Bank Connectivity

Required Function: Secure API and SFTP connections to your financial institutions for automated balance and transaction reporting from the previous day.

  • Real-time API connections
  • Secure SFTP file transfers
  • Previous day transaction data
  • Multi-bank account aggregation
  • Automated data synchronization

📊 Basic Forecasting Tools

Required Function: Manual forecast modeling capabilities with comprehensive variance analysis reporting to predict and track cash flow patterns.

  • Manual forecast creation
  • Variance analysis reporting
  • Historical trend analysis
  • Scenario planning tools
  • Forecast accuracy tracking

👁️ Cash Visibility

Required Function: Complete global view of all cash balances across accounts, including investment positions for comprehensive liquidity oversight.

  • Global cash position dashboard
  • Investment portfolio visibility
  • Multi-currency balance views
  • Real-time position updates
  • Consolidated reporting

🔍 Transaction Management

Required Function: Comprehensive tools to search, sort, tag, and group bank transactions for enhanced tracking, organization, and reporting capabilities.

  • Advanced search functionality
  • Custom sorting options
  • Transaction tagging system
  • Category grouping tools
  • Detailed reporting capabilities

Cash Tools vs. Advanced Systems

Cash Tools provide the foundation for treasury management with these four core functions. Companies requiring more advanced capabilities like treasury payments, market data feeds, or derivatives management would benefit from TMS-LITE or Treasury & Risk Management Systems (TRMS) solutions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about implementing cash management tools

What's the difference between Cash Tools and TMS-LITE systems?

Cash Tools include the four core functions: bank connectivity, basic forecasting, cash visibility, and transaction management. TMS-LITE systems include all Cash Tools functions plus cash positioning, market data feeds, and treasury payment capabilities.

Do I need all four functions to have a complete Cash Tool?

Yes, a product must have ALL four required functions to qualify as a complete Cash Tool: bank connectivity (API/SFTP), basic forecasting tools, cash visibility, and transaction search/sort/tag/group capabilities.

How accurate are basic forecasting tools in Cash Tools?

Basic forecasting tools in Cash Tools rely on manual modeling with variance analysis. While accuracy depends on user input quality, these tools typically achieve 80-90% accuracy for short-term forecasts when properly maintained.

Are Cash Tools suitable for companies with simple cash structures?

Absolutely! Cash Tools are specifically designed for companies with simple cash structures and straightforward treasury needs. They provide essential functionality without the complexity of advanced treasury systems.
